Office Manager
Location: Manchester Center, Vermont

The McBride Company is seeking an organized, friendly, and professional Office Manager (full-time and in-office) to help manage the day-to-day operations of our boutique interior and architectural design firm located in the heart of Manchester Center, Vermont.

Primary Responsibilities:

  • Serve as the point person for duties including:
    • Maintenance coordination
    • Mailing
    • Supplies
    • Bill payments
    • Travel coordination
    • Errands
    • Shopping
  • Contractual agreements
  • Schedule meetings and appointments
  • Organize the office layout and order stationery equipment
  • Maintain the office condition and arrange necessary repairs
  • Coordinate with local IT on the proper functioning of all office equipment
  • Manage employee timesheets (through an online system) and provide direct communications with employees to ensure accurate and timely billing cycles for project work with clients
  • Ensure that all items are invoiced and paid on time
  • Manage G&A budget, ensure accurate and timely reporting
  • Coordinate directly with the financial manager on accounts payable and accounts receivable

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Proven experience as an Office Manager or Administrative Assistant
  • Knowledge of Office Administrator responsibilities, systems, and procedures
  • Proficiency in MS Office Suite (Word and Excel in particular)
  • Hands-on experience with office machines (i.e. printers and scanners)
  • Familiarity with email scheduling tools
  • Experience with managing employee timesheets
  • Excellent time management skills and ability to multi-task and prioritize work
  • Attention to detail and problem-solving skills
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills
  • Strong organizational and planning skills in a fast-paced environment
  • A creative mind with an ability to suggest improvements
  • High School Degree at a minimum; additional qualifications will be a plus

Financial Administrator
Location: Manchester Center, VT

Renowned Manchester, VT-based creative design and innovation firm, The McBride Company, is seeking a highly experienced, full-time financial administrator and office manager that will be responsible for facilitating the functioning of the office via a range of administrative, clerical, financial, and managerial tasks.

Primary Responsibilities:

  • Tracking and monitoring the financial well-being of the company. This includes budgeting, forecasting, planning, and management of accounts payable, payroll, invoicing, and accounts receivable, as well as maintaining excellent relationships with both vendors and customers.
  • Regular review and analysis of P&L statements, balance sheets, and cash flow with management.
  • Contract Management including creation, tracking, insurance accords, and filing management.
  • Liaison to outside Insurance firms including a yearly review of renewals for Workers Comp, E&O, Business Owners policies.
  • Liaison to outside CPA firm including forecasting for state and federal tax liabilities.
  • Management of office operations and procedures such as correspondence, filing systems, managing timesheets, and reimbursable expenses

Experience Guidelines:

  • Degree in business, accounting, finance, or equivalent meaningful work experience in a related field
  • At least two years experience in accounting, business, bookkeeping, or related financial field
  • Proficiency in Quickbooks, ADP, Microsoft Office, GSuite, and data processing with strong attention to detail
  • Bonus points if you have experience working with an Interior Design or Architectural Firm or have experience with Replicon, Slack, BOX, Smartsheet, Adobe PDF, AIA Documents, PPP Loans, and Grants.
  • Excellent people skills and experience with customer service

Project Manager
Location: Vermont, Miami or Remote

Primary Responsibilities

We are seeking an organized and highly experienced PM responsible for overseeing large scale architectural projects in the fields of Hotel, Restaurant and Entertainment from budgeting through construction to project opening.

Primary Responsibilities

  • Help develop project schedules and budgets and ensure that these are adhered to through the process
  • Assist with budgeting, hiring and coordination of third party vendors including MEP, Engineering Consultants and Specialty Vendors
  • Help in the review and redlining of working architectural drawings including DD's and CD's ensuring they adapt to building codes and uphold the original program and intent
  • Answering or distribution of RFI's
  • Coordination of finished plans and specifications with ownership and GC's including management and approval of materials and construction throughout the construction process
  • Participate in on site supervision during the construction process
  • Regular travel to meetings and job sites

Experience Guidelines

  • At least 5 years of experience as a Project Manager in the architecture industry (specifically with experience in Hotels and Restaurants)
  • Knowledge of modern architectural methods with a background of solving architectural design and construction problems
  • Advanced computer knowledge and the ability to lead teams through excellent written and oral skills
